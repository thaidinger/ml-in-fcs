\begin{column}{0.40\textwidth}\scriptsize
\textbf{Data pipeline}
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}\setlength\topsep{0pt}\setlength\parskip{0pt}
  \item \textbf{Source:} Yahoo Finance daily close.
  \item \textbf{Modality:} \alert{raw prices}, not returns.
  \item \textbf{FTS-Diffusion split:} 80\,\% train / 20\,\% test.
  \item \textbf{Downstream LSTM:} carved from the 20\,\% test slice.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{2mm}
\begin{exampleblock}{Size asymmetry}
After segmentation, S\&P 500 is $\approx 3\times$ GOOG and $\approx 3.6\times$ ZC$=$F.
\end{exampleblock}

\note{Years come from the year span; days from the actual yfinance pull. The 80/20 split is the FTS-Diffusion training/test boundary; the downstream LSTM in TATR is fit on a disjoint 5y init slice of the held-out 20\%, then evaluated on the remaining $\approx\!3$y on S\&P 500. The same 62.5/37.5 proportion is kept for GOOG and ZC$=$F on their respective 20\% slices. We work on raw prices throughout (no return transform). After segmentation S\&P 500 is $\approx 3\times$ GOOG and $\approx 3.6\times$ ZC$=$F.}
