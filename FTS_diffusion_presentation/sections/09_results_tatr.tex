\vspace{1.5mm}
{\footnotesize FTS-Diffusion paper (Yu et al., ICLR 2024), Fig.~6(b): TATR prediction
error (MAPE) vs.\ augmented size --- FTS-Diffusion and three baselines (RCGAN, TimeGAN,
CSDI) on S\&P 500, GOOG and ZC$=$F. Dashed line $=$ year-0 MAPE.}
\note{The published TATR result, shown before our replication. The paper reports that FTS-Diffusion keeps the downstream MAPE flat or slightly below the year-0 baseline as the augmented size grows (headline $-17.9\%$), while the GAN/diffusion baselines drift up. This is the reference the next slides are compared against --- and note that our re-runs do \emph{not} reproduce the flat curve under the authors' protocol.}

\vspace{1mm}
\centering\footnotesize Replacing the deterministic re-init $(p_0,\alpha_0,\beta_0)$ with a random MC state:
\textcolor{Coral}{$+208.6\%$} $\rightarrow$ \textcolor{NavyBlue}{$+67.7\%$} --- the fixed seed \emph{hurts}.
\note{random\_init keeps the yearly re-init schedule but seeds each year from a random Markov state instead of the deterministic $(p_0,\alpha_0,\beta_0)$. It is markedly better than the authors' protocol here: the deterministic seed, not the MC dynamics, is what drags MAPE up. This is a diagnostic, not a recommended protocol.}
\end{frame}
