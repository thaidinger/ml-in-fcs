\vspace{0.5mm}
\begin{alertblock}{One question only}
Does adding $Y$ years of synthetic data \alert{reduce} the LSTM's MAPE on real held-out data?
A curve below the $Y\!=\!0$ baseline says \emph{yes}.
{\scriptsize 5y/3y are S\&P 500; the same 62.5/37.5 ratio is kept on GOOG and ZC$=$F.}
\end{alertblock}

\note{TATR is Train-on-Augmentation-Test-on-Real. The held-out 20\% slice of real history is split 62.5/37.5: the first 5 years anchor the LSTM training set, the last $\approx$3 years are the evaluation set (never seen by anything). FTS-Diffusion produces $Y$ years of synthetic data; we append them to the real init and train the LSTM (full-batch, 100 epochs, MAE, hidden=32, window=64). We sweep $Y$ from 0 (real-only baseline) to 100 years in 10-year steps. The metric is MAPE on the real test set, averaged over 15 seeds. \emph{How} those $Y$ synthetic years are produced is the protocol choice (next slide).}
